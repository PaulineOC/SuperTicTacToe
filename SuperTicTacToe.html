Multiplayer Super Tic Tac Toe

Rules:

Players take turns playing in the smaller tic-tac-toe boards until one of them wins in the larger tic-tac-toe board.

Each small 3-by-3 tic-tac-toe board is referred to as a local board, and the larger 3-by-3 board is referred to as the global board. Each move sends the opponent to its relative location on the global board. If an X is played in the top right square of a local board, then the O must be played in the top right local board of the global board. Here is a visual example move.

If a move is played so that it is to win a local board by the rules of normal tic-tac-toe, then the entire local board is marked as a victory for the player in the global board. Moves that send players to this won board are still valid.

Game play ends when either a player wins the global board, or there are no legal moves remaining.
